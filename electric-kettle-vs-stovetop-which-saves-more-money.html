Electric Kettle vs Stovetop: Which Saves More Money?

📅 April 28, 2026 • ⏱️ 6 min read • ✍️ KitchenWise Team

If you boil water daily for tea, coffee, or cooking, your method could be costing you more than you think. We spent a month measuring energy use, speed, and costs to settle the debate: electric kettle or stovetop?

The Math: Energy Costs in 2026

Based on the average US electricity rate of $0.16/kWh:

  • Electric Kettle (1500W): Boils 1 liter in 4 minutes = 0.1 kWh = $0.016 per boil
  • Gas Stovetop: Boils 1 liter in 7-9 minutes = ~0.15 kWh equivalent = $0.024 per boil
  • Electric Stovetop: Boils 1 liter in 8-10 minutes = 0.18 kWh = $0.029 per boil

The electric kettle wins by 30-45% on energy cost alone.

Speed Test Results

We timed 10 boils of 1 liter of room-temperature water:

  • Cuisinart PerfecTemp: 3 minutes 48 seconds average
  • Gas stovetop (high heat): 7 minutes 15 seconds average
  • Electric coil stove: 9 minutes 30 seconds average

The kettle is 2x faster than gas and 2.5x faster than electric coil. That time savings adds up to 15+ hours per year if you boil water twice daily.

Hidden Costs

Stovetop hidden costs:

  • Heat loss to the kitchen (raises AC costs in summer)
  • Pot replacement every 2-3 years ($20-40)
  • Gas line maintenance (for gas stoves)

Electric kettle hidden costs:

  • Descaling every 2 months (vinegar = $0.50)
  • Replacement every 4-5 years ($30-100)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it cheaper to boil water in a kettle or microwave?

Microwaves are slightly cheaper per boil (about $0.012), but they take longer and heat unevenly. Kettles are faster and safer.

Do electric kettles use a lot of electricity?

No. A typical 1500W kettle costs about $0.016 per boil. Even boiling 4 times daily, that's under $2 per month.

How often should I descale my kettle?

Every 2 months if you have hard water, every 4 months with soft water. Use white vinegar or citric acid.

Are glass kettles better than steel?

Glass lets you see the water level and looks modern, but steel retains heat longer and is more durable. We prefer steel for daily use.
