Your air fryer can do way more than frozen fries. We tested 40+ recipes across 3 different air fryers to find the ones that work every single time — no guesswork, no burnt food, no disappointment.

Each recipe includes exact temperature, time, and the one tip that makes it work. These were tested in a 5-quart basket air fryer. If yours is smaller, cook in batches. If larger, you might shave 1-2 minutes.

All recipes use little to no oil. A light spray of cooking oil (1-2 seconds) is optional for extra crispiness.

Perfect Frozen French Fries

⏱️ 18 min 🌡️ 400°F 🍽️ Serves 2-3 ⭐ Beginner
400°F | 18 minutes | Shake at 10 min
  1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F for 2 minutes (empty basket)
  2. Add frozen fries in a single layer — don't overcrowd
  3. Cook 10 minutes, then shake basket vigorously
  4. Cook 8 more minutes until golden and crispy
  5. Season immediately while hot (salt sticks better)
💡 Pro Tip: Don't thaw fries first. Straight from freezer to basket works best. Overcrowding = soggy fries. Cook in batches if needed.

Crispy Chicken Wings

⏱️ 25 min 🌡️ 380°F 🍽️ Serves 2-4 ⭐ Beginner
380°F | 25 minutes | Flip at 15 min
  1. Pat wings completely dry with paper towels (crucial for crispiness)
  2. Toss with 1 tsp baking powder + salt + pepper (no oil needed)
  3. Arrange in single layer, skin side up
  4. Cook 15 minutes, then flip each wing
  5. Cook 10 more minutes until skin is crackling
  6. Toss in sauce AFTER cooking (sauce burns if added early)
💡 Pro Tip: Baking powder (not soda) raises the pH of skin, making it ultra-crispy. Sounds weird, works magic.

Air Fryer Bacon

⏱️ 10 min 🌡️ 350°F 🍽️ Serves 2 ⭐ Beginner
350°F | 10 minutes | Check at 8 min
  1. Cut bacon strips in half (fits better, cooks more evenly)
  2. Arrange in single layer — slight overlap is OK
  3. Cook 8 minutes, check for desired crispiness
  4. Cook 2 more minutes if you like it extra crispy
  5. Drain on paper towels for 1 minute
💡 Pro Tip: Place a slice of bread under the basket to catch grease drips. No smoke, no mess, easy cleanup.

Roasted Vegetables

⏱️ 15 min 🌡️ 375°F 🍽️ Serves 3-4 ⭐ Beginner
375°F | 15 minutes | Shake at 8 min
  1. Chop broccoli, cauliflower, or Brussels sprouts into similar sizes
  2. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder
  3. Arrange in single layer — don't stack
  4. Cook 8 minutes, shake basket
  5. Cook 7 more minutes until edges are charred
  6. Squeeze lemon juice over top before serving
💡 Pro Tip: Cut pieces the same size or smaller ones burn while larger ones stay raw. 1-inch pieces are the sweet spot.

Reheated Pizza (Better Than Fresh)

⏱️ 4 min 🌡️ 350°F 🍽️ Serves 1 ⭐ Beginner
350°F | 4 minutes | No preheat needed
  1. Place leftover pizza slice(s) in basket — don't overlap
  2. Cook 3 minutes (crust starts crisping)
  3. Check — if cheese isn't bubbling, cook 1 more minute
  4. Remove immediately (continues cooking in basket)
💡 Pro Tip: This is the #1 reason to own an air fryer. Microwave makes pizza rubbery. Oven takes 15 minutes. Air fryer = crispy crust, melty cheese, 4 minutes.
