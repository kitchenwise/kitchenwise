What Kills Non-Stick Pans

  • High heat: Never above medium. High heat degrades the coating.
  • Metal utensils: Use wood, silicone, or nylon only.
  • Abrasive cleaners: No steel wool or scouring pads.
  • Stacking without protection: Pan surfaces touching each other scratch the coating.

When to Replace

If you see scratches exposing the metal underneath, replace immediately. Flaking coating can end up in your food. A good non-stick pan lasts 2-3 years with proper care.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are non-stick pans safe?

Modern non-stick (PTFE/Teflon) is safe below 500°F. PFOA has been banned since 2015. If you see scratches, replace the pan to avoid ingesting coating flakes.

Can I use non-stick pans in the oven?

Most are oven-safe to 350°F. Check the handle material — plastic handles can't go in the oven. The T-fal set is oven-safe to 400°F.

Is ceramic non-stick better than Teflon?

Ceramic is more eco-friendly but less durable. It loses non-stick properties faster. For longevity, hard-anodized aluminum with PTFE coating is still best.

How do I make non-stick pans last longer?

Never use high heat, never use metal utensils, hand wash when possible, and store with pan protectors or paper towels between pans.
